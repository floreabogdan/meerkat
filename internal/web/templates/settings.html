{{template "icon-ban"}}Blocking

meerkat does not block anything itself, and Suricata here is deliberately alert-only — left at its defaults it dropped 9.6% of transit traffic on this router, and not because of any rule. Dropping is nftables' job, so a block from meerkat is a call to nftably's firewall API.

Where nftably is listening. Loopback is right when both run on the same router.
Mint it in nftably under Settings → Automation API. Until a token exists there, its /api/block answers 404 rather than 401 — the feature is not merely locked, it is off. Leave blank to keep the token already stored.

Run meerkat doctor after saving: it probes this endpoint and reports exactly which of "not configured", "no token", "wrong token" or "API disabled" you are looking at.

Two things never leave this box, by construction: the destination address — the map shows a site name and a port, because publishing which of our hosts are live and being probed is free reconnaissance — and any source inside our own networks, since an infected internal machine calling out trips a rule with our address as the source.
